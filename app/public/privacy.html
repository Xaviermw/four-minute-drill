Privacy

Last updated July 5, 2026

Four Minute Drill is a free daily football game. It collects as little as it can, has no ads, and sells nothing. Here is exactly what it does and doesn't do with data.

What's stored

  • The display name and score you choose to submit to a public leaderboard. These are visible to everyone by design — that's the leaderboard.
  • An anonymous account id — a random identifier (no email, no login) so your own scores can be highlighted as yours and your win streak can be tracked.
  • Anonymous gameplay analytics (via Vercel Analytics): aggregate events like "a drive was completed" or "a result was shared," used to understand how the game is played. No personal profiles.
  • Local settings stored in your browser (localStorage): your day streak, whether you've seen the intro, and the last name you entered. These never leave your device unless you submit a score.

What's not

  • No email address, real name, or account password is ever requested.
  • No advertising, no third-party tracking pixels, no cross-site profiling.
  • Nothing is sold or shared with data brokers.

Deleting your data

Want a leaderboard entry removed? Email xavier.weisenreder@gmail.com with the display name you used and it'll be taken down. You can clear your local settings anytime by clearing this site's browser data.

Player data

Player stats and outcomes come from public NFL play-by-play data (nflverse / nflfastR). The game uses no team logos or player photos.
